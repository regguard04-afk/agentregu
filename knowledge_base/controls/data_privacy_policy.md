# Data Privacy Policy

**Control ID:** DP-001  
**Version:** 2.3  
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-15  
**Owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO)  
**Classification:** Internal  

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for protecting personal data throughout its lifecycle, ensuring compliance with global data privacy regulations including GDPR, India's DPDP Act, and other applicable laws.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all processing of personal data by the organization, including data collected from customers, employees, vendors, and business partners across all jurisdictions.

## 3. Data Privacy Principles

### 3.1 Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
- All data processing must have a valid legal basis (consent, contract, legal obligation, legitimate interest, vital interest, or public interest).
- Privacy notices must be provided before or at the time of data collection.
- Processing purposes must be clearly communicated to data subjects.

### 3.2 Purpose Limitation
- Personal data collected for specified purposes shall not be processed for incompatible purposes.
- New processing purposes require fresh legal basis assessment.
- Marketing use requires explicit opt-in consent.

### 3.3 Data Minimization
- Collect only the minimum personal data necessary for the stated purpose.
- Regular reviews to identify and eliminate unnecessary data fields.
- Default settings must be privacy-protective (privacy by default).

### 3.4 Accuracy
- Mechanisms for data subjects to update their personal information.
- Periodic data quality reviews for critical datasets.
- Correction requests processed within 30 days.

### 3.5 Storage Limitation
- Personal data retained only as long as necessary for the processing purpose.
- Retention schedules aligned with Data Retention Policy (DR-001).
- Anonymization applied to data needed for analytics beyond retention period.

## 4. Data Subject Rights

| Right | Response SLA | Process |
|---|---|---|
| Access (SAR) | 30 days | Privacy team via data-privacy@company.com |
| Rectification | 30 days | Direct update or privacy team request |
| Erasure (Right to be Forgotten) | 30 days | Privacy impact assessment + deletion |
| Portability | 30 days | Machine-readable export (JSON/CSV) |
| Objection | 30 days | Cease processing + legal review |
| Restrict Processing | Without delay | Flag records, halt processing |
| Withdraw Consent | Without delay | Update consent records, cease processing |

## 5. Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs)

- DPIA required for all new processing activities involving personal data.
- DPIA required for changes to existing high-risk processing.
- DPIA must be completed before processing begins.
- DPO review and sign-off required for all DPIAs.

## 6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

- Data transfers outside EEA require adequate safeguards (SCCs, BCRs, or adequacy decision).
- Transfer impact assessments required for transfers to non-adequate jurisdictions.
- Data localization requirements tracked per jurisdiction (India, China, Russia).
- Cloud service agreements must specify data residency.

## 7. Consent Management

- Granular consent options (not bundled).
- Consent records maintained with timestamp, version, and scope.
- Easy-to-use mechanism for withdrawing consent.
- Consent renewal for processing purpose changes.

## 8. Privacy by Design

- Privacy considerations integrated into all system design and development processes.
- Default privacy settings must be the most protective.
- Privacy architecture review for all new applications and systems.
- Data flow mapping maintained for all personal data processing.

## 9. Breach Notification (Personal Data)

- Privacy breach assessment completed within 24 hours of detection.
- DPA notification within 72 hours where required (GDPR).
- Data subject notification without undue delay for high-risk breaches.
- Coordinate with Incident Response Policy (IR-001).
