# Vendor Risk Management Policy

**Control ID:** VR-001  
**Version:** 2.0  
**Last Updated:** 2025-12-01  
**Owner:** Procurement & Risk Team  
**Classification:** Internal  

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for assessing, monitoring, and managing risks associated with third-party vendors, service providers, and business partners who have access to organizational data or systems.

## 2. Scope

All third-party relationships involving access to organizational data, systems, or facilities, including cloud service providers, SaaS vendors, outsourced service providers, and consultants.

## 3. Vendor Classification

| Tier | Criteria | Assessment Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Access to sensitive data, core business dependency | Annually + continuous monitoring |
| High | Access to internal data, significant operational role | Annually |
| Medium | Limited data access, replaceable service | Every 2 years |
| Low | No data access, minimal operational impact | At onboarding only |

## 4. Due Diligence Requirements

### 4.1 Pre-Engagement Assessment
- Security questionnaire (SIG or equivalent) for Tier Critical and High vendors.
- SOC 2 Type II report review (or equivalent certification).
- Financial stability assessment for Critical vendors.
- Legal review of data processing agreements.
- Reference checks for new Critical vendors.

### 4.2 Contractual Requirements
- Data processing agreements (DPA) aligned with GDPR/applicable regulations.
- Right to audit clause for Critical and High tier vendors.
- Incident notification clause (breach notification within 24 hours).
- Data return and destruction obligations upon contract termination.
- Subprocessor approval requirements.
- SLA definitions with remedies for non-compliance.

### 4.3 Ongoing Monitoring
- Annual security assessment for Critical and High vendors.
- Continuous monitoring of vendor security posture (security ratings).
- Review of vendor SOC reports upon receipt.
- Track vendor-related incidents and near-misses.
- Monitor vendor financial health indicators for Critical vendors.

## 5. Fourth-Party Risk
- Critical vendors must disclose material subprocessors.
- Subprocessor changes require prior notification.
- Right to assess fourth-party controls for Critical data flows.

## 6. Vendor Exit Strategy
- Documented exit plan for all Critical vendors.
- Data migration testing conducted annually.
- Transition period and support requirements defined in contracts.
- Data destruction verification upon relationship termination.

## 7. Compliance References
